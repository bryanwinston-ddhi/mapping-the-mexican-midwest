Mapping the Mexican Midwest

The map below visualizes Mexican communities and institutions in the Lower Midwest from 1920 to 1940.

    How to use the map

  • Toggle between communities, churches, and consular organizations using the layer control in the top right corner.
  • By clicking on a marker, a popup will appear with more identifying information about what the marker represents.

    An explanation of terms

  • Communities include neighborhoods in metropolitan areas, small cities, and rural towns.
  • Churches include religious classification and if possible, identify the national identity.
  • Consular organizations not only include the Mexican consulates of Kansas City, Missouri and St. Louis, Missouri, but also the many Comisiones Honorificas Mexicanas and Brigadas de la Cruz Azul.
